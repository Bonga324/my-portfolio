5 Things I Learned from Building a Travel Wishlist App

Creating my travel wishlist app was more than just a fun project — it was a great exercise in user-centric thinking and interface design. The goal was to inspire and help users plan their dream vacations to places like New York, Tokyo, and Paris. Here are the top five lessons I took away from the process:

  • 1. Design for Emotion: Travel is emotional. I used vibrant images and compelling copy to spark excitement and wanderlust in users.
  • 2. Usability Over Everything: Even beautiful features are useless if they confuse people. I focused on making navigation and budget tools intuitive and simple.
  • 3. Interactive Features Make It Memorable: Adding a money calculator and live polls turned the app from passive to interactive — users love being part of the experience.
  • 4. Responsive Design Is a Must: Many users visit travel apps on phones. I ensured the experience was just as smooth on small screens as on desktop.
  • 5. Listen to Feedback: Testers pointed out things I had missed, like unclear buttons and confusing icons. Their feedback made the app significantly better.

Overall, building this app taught me how to balance aesthetics with practicality, and how even small tweaks can make a big impact. Every feature should earn its place by adding real value.
