Behind the Code: Building eConFix

Building eConFix was a deep dive into real-world problem-solving. The goal was to connect users with skilled technicians for a wide range of construction and maintenance services — all through an easy-to-use online platform.

I started by designing a clean interface where users could quickly browse services, get estimates, and book appointments. Using HTML, CSS, and Bootstrap, I ensured the layout was responsive and accessible.

On the backend, I used ASP.NET MVC to handle service requests, user authentication, and secure online payments. One of the biggest challenges was coordinating multiple services like plumbing, HVAC, and security — all while keeping the user journey smooth and intuitive.

This project taught me how to manage complex service logic, integrate third-party tools, and focus on UX even when things get technically complex. Most importantly, it reminded me that good software always puts people first.
