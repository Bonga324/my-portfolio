5 Things I Learned from Building a Travel Wishlist App

Blog & Insights • Lessons Learned

Back to Blog View Projects

Creating my travel wishlist app was more than just a fun project — it was a great exercise in user-centric thinking and interface design. The goal was to inspire and help users plan their dream vacations to places like New York, Tokyo, and Paris.

  • 1. Design for Emotion: Travel is emotional. I used vibrant imagery and strong copy to spark excitement and wanderlust.
  • 2. Usability Over Everything: I focused on navigation clarity and intuitive budget tools.
  • 3. Interactive Features Make It Memorable: A budget calculator and polls helped turn the experience into something engaging.
  • 4. Responsive Design Is a Must: Many visitors come from phones, so smooth mobile UX mattered from day one.
  • 5. Listen to Feedback: Testing feedback improved clarity of buttons, layout, and interactions.

This project taught me how to balance aesthetics with practicality — and how small UX changes can create a big impact.
